# Project Summary & Consortium Structure

## TURBINE INSPECTION FORM v0.1
### Applied Industrial Auditing & Diagnostics System (Audityzer)

---

## 📋 Project Summary Paragraph (for Grant Application)

**Audityzer Turbine Inspection Form v0.1** is a comprehensive, digitalized field inspection framework designed for comprehensive technical diagnostics of energy generation equipment (steam and gas turbines, generators) across industrial facilities, power plants, and energy complexes in Ukraine and Central Asia. The project delivers:

- **Modular field specification** with 40+ structured data fields organized by turbine type (steam/gas-specific) and equipment categories (node status, measurements, control systems, media documentation)
- **Scalable open-source architecture** enabling systematic field extension without disrupting base functionality
- **Multi-platform support** (web, mobile-optimized for offline field operations)
- **Structured workflow automation** for inspection scheduling, data collection, risk assessment, and compliance reporting
- **Enterprise-grade framework** supporting government oversight, R&D validation, industrial asset management, and equipment manufacturers' compliance verification

The system bridges critical gaps in energy sector infrastructure assessment by standardizing turbine inspection procedures, enabling data-driven predictive maintenance, reducing equipment downtime, and supporting Ukraine's energy security and industrial modernization initiatives. Initial version (v0.1) establishes sustainable foundation for progressive enhancement through structured consortium collaboration and community-driven development.

## 🤝 Consortium Structure & Roles

### **Consortium Governance Model**

The Audityzer project employs a **multi-stakeholder collaborative consortium** ensuring balanced representation, transparent decision-making, and sustainable development across public, academic, and private sectors.

---

## 1️⃣ GOVERNMENT & PUBLIC SECTOR (Policy Steering Committee)

### **Primary Roles & Institutions**

#### 🏛️ **Ministry of Energy** (Lead Policy Partner)
- **Role**: Strategic oversight, energy security alignment, regulatory framework development
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Define technical standards for turbine inspection across state energy facilities
  - Establish compliance requirements for thermal power plants and combined heat/power stations
  - Integrate Audityzer into national energy infrastructure monitoring protocols
  - Support data sharing between government energy assets and diaspora/foreign partners
- **Contribution**: Policy guidance, regulatory approval, state facility access for pilot testing

#### 🏢 **State Service for Efficient Energy Use (SAEE)**
- **Role**: Implementation coordination, energy efficiency standards integration
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Align inspection protocols with efficiency certification requirements
  - Support pilot deployment across state-owned industrial enterprises
  - Provide baseline data for turbine performance benchmarking
- **Contribution**: Technical advisory, facility partnerships, standardization support

#### 🏛️ **National Commission for State Regulation of Energy** (NERC)
- **Role**: Regulatory validation, compliance framework development
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Validate technical specifications against international energy standards
  - Establish regulatory requirements for equipment monitoring
  - Define data accessibility standards for audits and compliance reporting
- **Contribution**: Regulatory expertise, standards alignment

#### 🛡️ **State Emergency Service / Industrial Safety Directorate**
- **Role**: Safety compliance integration, risk management standardization
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Incorporate safety inspection protocols into form structure
  - Define risk escalation procedures for critical equipment defects
  - Support emergency response coordination through inspection data
- **Contribution**: Safety standards, defect classification methodology

## 5️⃣ COORDINATING STRUCTURE

### **Project Governance**

```
STEERING COMMITTEE
├── Government (Ministry of Energy)
├── Academia (KPI, IEC representative)
├── Business (State-owned energy company)
└── Civil Society (UAEE representative)
    ↓
TECHNICAL WORKING GROUPS
├── Form Specification & Fields
├── Platform Development & Testing
├── Data Standards & Interoperability
├── Training & Competency Development
└── Regulatory & Compliance Integration
```

### **Decision-Making Process**
1. **Monthly Technical Reviews**: Technical working groups review field additions and code changes
2. **Quarterly Steering Sessions**: Strategic decisions on priorities, partnerships, funding
3. **Annual General Assembly**: Formal approval of roadmap, version releases, consortium updates
4. **Open Community Feedback**: GitHub Issues and public discussions for transparency

### **Conflict Resolution**
- Technical disputes resolved by Steering Committee with expert consultant input
- Commercial interests balanced through open forum discussion
- Public interest (government/civil society) maintains final approval on regulatory impacts
